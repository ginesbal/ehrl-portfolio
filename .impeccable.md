# Impeccable Design Context

Persistent design guidelines for Ehrl Balquin's portfolio. All design work — current and future — should flow from this document.

## Design Context

### Users

The portfolio serves three audiences simultaneously, weighted equally:

- **Hiring managers & recruiters** skim fast and need instant signals of credibility, fit, and shipped work. They rarely read body copy in full.
- **Technical interviewers & engineering peers** want depth — architecture decisions, trade-offs, code-level honesty. They follow links to dedicated project pages (ParkPal, EVision).
- **Design-conscious clients & agencies** judge the portfolio itself as proof of craft. The interface must stand on its own as a designed object.

The portfolio's job-to-be-done: communicate "this person ships thoughtful, well-built software" in under 10 seconds, while rewarding longer attention with substance.

### Brand Personality

**Calm. Editorial. Precise.**

- *Calm* — generous whitespace, restrained motion, no fighting for attention
- *Editorial* — typography-led hierarchy, print-influenced rhythm, the work is the subject
- *Precise* — considered spacing, consistent tokens, invisible-details-compound discipline

Evoke confidence and trust, not hype. The site should feel made, not assembled.

### Aesthetic Direction

**Anchor references (emulate the feel):**
- Emil Kowalski's personal site — meticulous motion, invisible details
- Rauno Freiberg — craft-first, restrained palette, typography as architecture
- Vercel's marketing / design-engineer portfolio pages — clean structure, quiet sophistication

**Anti-references (explicitly avoid):**
- Developer-bro dark-mode-with-neon sites
- Glassmorphism-heavy "SaaS template" aesthetics
- Identical-card portfolio grids
- Bounce/elastic easing, gradient-text hero headings, "AI-slop" tropes

**Palette:** warm editorial — taupe / parchment / rose-taupe (`#9a4a32`) / olive-parchment (`#7b7451`). Light mode is primary; dark mode exists as an optional secondary theme, not the default. Neutrals are tinted toward the rose-taupe hue for subconscious cohesion.

**Typography direction:** Inter for body and UI chrome. **Fraunces** (Undercase Type) for large headings and editorial accents — a modulated serif with restrained italics that reads calm, editorial, and precise. Avoid fashion-magazine serifs (Instrument Serif, Playfair Display) — their dramatic contrast and flourished italics clash with the "precise" brand attribute. The pairing — precise geometric sans with warm, slightly quirky editorial serif — signals "engineer with taste."

**Theme:** Light by default. The warm beige ground (`--bg-primary: #f3f2f1`) is intentional — it reads as paper, not screen.

### Design Principles

1. **Invisible details compound.** Motion, spacing, and timing decisions should be imperceptible individually but unmistakable in aggregate. Emil Kowalski's rules apply: no `transition: all`, buttons must feel pressed, never animate from `scale(0)`, exit always faster than enter.

2. **Typography is the architecture.** Hierarchy comes from type scale, tracking, and weight — not from drop shadows, gradients, or boxes. Headings in the display serif; everything else in Inter with considered weights.

3. **Editorial rhythm over uniform grids.** Break the grid intentionally. Don't wrap everything in cards. Not every section needs the same padding. Asymmetry and varied rhythm feel designed; monotony feels templated.

4. **Warm neutrals, single accent.** Rose-taupe is the only saturated color that earns its place. Parchment is a secondary accent used sparingly. No gradients on metrics, no rainbow, no neon — the palette commits.

5. **Light by default, tinted always.** No pure black (`#000`) or pure white (`#fff`). Every neutral carries a hint of the brand hue. Dark mode is a courtesy, not the hero.

6. **Refine, don't rebuild.** This portfolio has an established voice. Every pass should sharpen what's here, not redirect it. Changes are measured against "does this align with calm/editorial/precise?"
